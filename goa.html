Goa

Goa, nestled on the southwestern coast of India, is renowned for its vibrant blend of Portuguese and Indian cultures. Famous for its pristine beaches, such as Anjuna and Baga, Goa attracts travelers with its stunning coastal scenery and lively nightlife. The region's historic architecture, including colonial-era churches and forts, adds a touch of old-world charm. Goa's culinary scene, marked by spicy seafood and unique fusion dishes, offers a delightful culinary adventure. Beyond the beaches, lush forests and picturesque backwaters invite exploration.

MUST-visit Destinations:


Baga Beach

Known for its vibrant nightlife, water sports, and bustling shacks, Baga Beach is a popular destination for party-goers and beach lovers.

Dudhsagar Falls:

A majestic waterfall located in the Western Ghats, Dudhsagar is a must-visit for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

Fort Aguada

This historic fort, overlooking the Arabian Sea, offers panoramic views and is a great spot for history enthusiasts.

Basilica of Bom Jesus

A UNESCO World Heritage site, this church houses the mortal remains of St. Francis Xavier and is an architectural marvel with its Baroque style.


Goa Tour Packages

Low Budget Packages

Price Range: ₹10,000 - ₹25,000 per person (for a 3-4 day trip)

  • Inclusions:
    • Accommodation: Budget hotels or guesthouses
    • Meals: Basic (often just breakfast)
    • Transport: Shared transport or economy options
    • Sightseeing: Group tours with visits to major attractions
  • Typical Itinerary:
    • Day 1: Arrival and check-in. Visit Calangute Beach and Baga Beach.
    • Day 2: Explore Old Goa, including the Basilica of Bom Jesus and Se Cathedral. Afternoon visit to Anjuna Beach.
    • Day 3: Visit Dudhsagar Waterfalls (often as part of a group tour). Optionally explore the local markets.
    • Day 4: Check out and departure. Free time for last-minute shopping or a final beach visit.

Mid Budget Packages

Price Range: ₹25,000 - ₹50,000 per person (for a 4-5 day trip)

  • Inclusions:
    • Accommodation: 3-star hotels or boutique stays
    • Meals: Breakfast and sometimes additional meals (lunch or dinner)
    • Transport: Private or semi-private transport
    • Sightseeing: Curated tours with some exclusivity
  • Typical Itinerary:
    • Day 1: Arrival and check-in. Evening visit to Candolim Beach and a sunset cruise on the Mandovi River.
    • Day 2: Explore Old Goa, including the Basilica of Bom Jesus, Se Cathedral, and St. Augustine Tower. Afternoon visit to Miramar Beach.
    • Day 3: Full-day tour to Dudhsagar Waterfalls with lunch included. Option for a jeep safari or trek.
    • Day 4: Visit North Goa beaches like Anjuna, Vagator, and Chapora Fort. Optional evening visit to a beach shack or club.
    • Day 5: Check out and departure. Free time for shopping or relaxing at a beach.

High Budget Packages

Price Range: ₹50,000 and above per person (for a 5-7 day trip)

  • Inclusions:
    • Accommodation: 4-5 star hotels or luxury resorts
    • Meals: All meals (buffet or à la carte)
    • Transport: Private luxury vehicle with chauffeur
    • Sightseeing: Customized tours with private guides
  • Typical Itinerary:
    • Day 1: Arrival and check-in at a luxury resort. Relaxing afternoon at the resort’s private beach or pool. Evening dinner at a fine-dining restaurant.
    • Day 2: Private tour of Old Goa, visiting the Basilica of Bom Jesus, Se Cathedral, and St. Cajetan Church. Afternoon at Panjim for a river cruise and visit to local markets.
    • Day 3: Full-day private tour to Dudhsagar Waterfalls with an optional helicopter ride or luxury jeep safari. Gourmet lunch included.
    • Day 4: Private beach tour to North Goa beaches such as Anjuna, Vagator, and Ashwem Beach. Evening at a high-end beach club or resort.
    • Day 5: Visit to a spice plantation with a guided tour and traditional Goan lunch. Option for a private cooking class or cultural experience.
    • Day 6: Leisure day with optional activities like yoga, spa treatments, or a private yacht charter.
    • Day 7: Check out and departure. Time for last-minute shopping or beach relaxation before departure.
Way to Travel:



  • By Air✈️

    • By Air: Dabolim Airport (GOI) is the primary airport serving Goa, located in Dabolim. It is well-connected with major Indian cities like Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore, and Chennai, as well as some international destinations. It’s the fastest and most convenient way to reach Goa.
  • By Train 🚆

  • By Train: Goa has several railway stations, with the most prominent ones being Madgaon (Margao), Vasco da Gama, and Thivim. Trains from major cities like Mumbai, Pune, and Bangalore connect Goa efficiently. The Konkan Railway route offers scenic views along the coast.

  • By Road 🛣

    • Self-Drive or Car Rentals:
  • you prefer road trips, you can drive to Goa from nearby cities. The drive from Mumbai or Pune, for instance, is quite popular and offers scenic views of the Western Ghats and coastal regions. Renting a car or hiring a taxi is also a viable option for a more personalized journey.
