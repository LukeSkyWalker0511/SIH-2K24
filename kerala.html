Kerala

Kerala, situated on the southwestern coast of India, is celebrated for its stunning natural beauty and diverse cultural heritage. Known as "God's Own Country," it is renowned for its lush green landscapes, tranquil backwaters, and pristine beaches. The state features a unique geography with the Western Ghats on one side and the Arabian Sea on the other, creating a diverse range of ecosystems. Kerala's rich cultural tapestry includes classical dance forms like Kathakali and Mohiniyattam, vibrant festivals, and a rich tradition of literature and art. It also stands out for its high literacy rate and emphasis on social welfare, contributing to its reputation as one of the most progressive states in India. With a focus on sustainable tourism and eco-friendly practices, Kerala attracts visitors from around the world who seek both relaxation and cultural enrichment.

MUST-visit Destinations:


Munnar:

A scenic hill station known for its sprawling tea gardens, cool climate, and picturesque landscapes, including the Anamudi Peak, the highest point in South India.

Kumarakom:

Another backwater destination with luxury resorts and houseboats, renowned for its bird sanctuary and peaceful waterways.

Kochi (Cochin):

A vibrant city known for its historic sites like Fort Kochi, the Dutch Palace, and the Chinese fishing nets, as well as its lively arts scene and colonial architecture.

Varkala:

A coastal town with stunning cliffs overlooking the Arabian Sea, known for its beautiful beaches and vibrant cliff-side cafes.
